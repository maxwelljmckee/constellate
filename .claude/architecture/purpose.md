# Why build Audri?

Audri is a voice-first AI assistant. Here's what makes it different from other AI products:

## Problem Statements

### Continuity

The mistake most AI applications make is that their "memory" features are just not powerful enough to meet real-world needs. Even if it has a superficial knowledge of who I am and what I'm working on, it still _feels like_ each session starts completely fresh, with no carry-over in context from previous conversations.

A truly future-looking AI product has a deep knowledge of who we are; our goals and values, our projects and work, relationships, everything. To be paradigm-shifting in impact and utility, it needs to be deeply interwoven into the fabric of our lives. Each session with an AI should carry the context of everything it knows about me, recent conversations.

### Discoverability

In Ecology, invasive species are plants or animals that enter into a non-native habitat. Often, this leads to a population explosion for the invasive plant or animal. Here's the really interesting part: often, the reason for the population explosion is not that the invasive species has no natural predators, rather it's because viable predators have not yet learned to recognize the invasive species _as prey_!

What does this have to do with AI? Right now, the only users who have begun to scratch the surface of the vast potential of AI are technical power users. Coders are having a great time, but many "lay" folks are struggling to figure out how it's useful to them. That's because it's too foreign. It falls too far outside their existing mental models for how to interact with technology for it to be intuitive. What they need is a **translation layer**; something that exposes AI's powerful capabilities to them in an idiom they can understand.

### Centrality

Our digital identities are fragmented. Of the dozens of services we use, each of them has an awareness of some small sliver of our lives and preferences. Having our digital lives split across so many different services is exhausting, not to mention the "digital maintenance" task of ensuring they are all secured and up to date.

Current AI applications feel like bolt-ons to existing software. The shape of the digital landscape has not yet changed, nor has the underlying service. All that's changed is our means of interacting with the existing services (and somewhat cosmetically at that).

We need a "glue" product. Something that sits in the middle of all the fragments of our digital lives and holds it all together.
